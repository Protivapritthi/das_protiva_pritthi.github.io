Research Projects

  • Cyberbiosecurity and Bio-LLM Security: Ongoing research on improving the security of biological large language models against dual-use misuse and multi-turn jailbreak attacks. This work investigates how Bio-LLMs may be manipulated through multi-step conversational strategies and explores defense mechanisms for safer deployment in sensitive biological domains.
  • BioShield: A Context-Aware Firewall for Securing Bio-LLMs: A research project focused on designing a context-aware defense layer for Bio-LLMs that can detect and block unsafe conversational trajectories, especially in cyberbiosecurity-sensitive settings.
